Designing an Icon

This project was my first to involve design thinking structured into mulitple iterative steps and lots of critical, creative thinking. We were tasked to design an app icon that was reflective of a function based on a subject prompt given to every single student. In my case, I had to design an app icon that “intentionally gets you lost”. The primary objective of this assignment was to focus on integrating design principles into an icon that conveyed the function of the application.

Step 1

mindmapping

The first step in the iterative process was to sketch out anything related to my app concept. As someone who was (and is) very new to the process of design thinking, I wrote down anything that expressed emotions, objects and simple notions that related to the key words of my concept. I had to ensure that the symbolic meaning of the icons were reflective of the intentions within the app function.

Step 2

sketching

The second step was involved sketching and implementing the key objects and symbols that reflected the notions of “getting lost” based on the ideas generated from my mindmap.By using symbols such as arrows and question marks and combining them with other symbolic objects, like the compass, my sketches allowed me to flush out any over-used icons and vere towards an app which was more creative in reflecting its vital function.

Step 3

refining icons

After picking three of my favoruite icons in the sketches and refining them, I ran into my first problems as a designer. Upon sharing my work with others, I was told that the function of the app was not entirely reflective of my concept and that perhaps by mixing and matching some of the elements already present in my icons I’d be able to successfully convey the message of getting lost intentionally. Though these designs weren’t entirely unsuccessful, I knew there was more I could do to push forward with the process.

Step 4

refining continued

This is where the magic began to happen. With the help of some peer feedback, I re-mixed the original concept of the compass and the man to create something more representative of an app that intentionally gets you lost. In turn, I felt more confident with my design sketches because of the direction I was given.

Step 5

final phase

In the final step there was more to it than just simply creating the icon. Instead of sticking to my original design sketch, I experimented with various stroke weights and gray tones to achieve the desired look. I learnt that not every design decision has to be explicitly deliberate to the viewer but rather gives just enough to communicate the message.
